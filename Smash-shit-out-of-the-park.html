Objective 01

Increase Contact Rate

Getting more prospects to answer or re-engage. Stop wasting dead dials.

01

Book Proper Times and Dates

No vague callbacks. Every call ends with a specific booked callback — date and time confirmed.

02

Change Phone Number After 6 Dials

After 6 missed attempts from the same number, rotate the outbound number to avoid fatigue and being ignored.

03

6 No Answers → Eva Calls

Eva becomes the reactivation route after 6 no answers. A fresh contact attempt from a different voice and angle.

04

Analyse LOA Age

Older LOAs may have weaker intent. Understand whether LOA age is affecting answer rates and prioritise accordingly.

Objective 02

Increase DMC Conversion

Ash: 12.5% | Charley: 6.5%. Improve what happens once contact is made. Turn contact into meaningful DMCs.

01

Side-by-Side Coaching

Spend a full day with each closer. Hear calls in real time, see behaviour, identify exactly where opportunities are lost.

02

Business-Specific Proposals

Stop sending generic quotes. Proposals must reflect the customer's business, situation, risk, contract end date, usage, and decision factors.

03

Better Notes

Notes must show what was actually learned: what matters, who the decision maker is, what the objection was, and what the next step is.

04

Business Questions

Show genuine interest. Not just a price — proper business questions that make the customer feel understood.

05

Discovery Questions

Find pain, priorities, decision process, timing, current concerns, previous experience, and what would make them move forward.

06

Close on the Call

Stop ending calls with 'I'll send it over.' Make a proper attempt to secure agreement on every call.

07

Get Commitment

Every call ends with a commitment: a decision, booked next step, Teams call, document return, or clear agreement.

Acquisition

Create Belief

"You gave us authority to review the account. Now we need to show you whether staying as you are is the best option, or whether there is a better commercial route."

Renewal

Protect & Retain

"You already know us. The question is whether we have done enough to keep your business and protect you properly going forward."
01

Separate Acquisition & Renewal Properly

They are not the same sale. Different psychology, different approach.

02

Prioritise the Right Opportunities

Focus on CED proximity, LOA age, business size, supplier, previous contact quality.

03

Improve Proposal Quality

Explain current position, market movement, risk of delay, options, and next steps.

04

Use Teams Calls

For bigger businesses. More control, professionalism, easier proposal explanation, higher commitment.

05

Tighter Follow-Up

"No proposal without a booked follow-up. 'I'll send this now — let's go through it together at 2:30.'"

06

Better Objection Handling

Push back on: 'I'll think about it', 'Send it over', 'I need to speak to my partner', 'We're happy where we are', 'It seems expensive', 'I'll wait.'

07

Close for the Next Step

If not the contract, close something: a Teams meeting, decision date, second stakeholder call, permission to proceed, documents back.

08

Daily Pipeline Review

Short daily review: who contacted, who became DMC, who received a proposal, who had a booked next step, who was closed, where control was lost.

Summary

In Plain English

You are using Ash and Charley as a controlled improvement project. The aim is to prove that if they contact people more intelligently, stop wasting dead dials, use Eva as a recovery route, understand LOA age, ask better questions, build business-specific proposals, take better notes, try to close properly, and secure clear commitments — then contact rate should move from 46% to 60%+, DMC conversion should improve from 12.5% and 6.5%, and that should then increase both acquisition and renewal conversion.

Do Not Let This Become a 'Work Harder' Project

It needs to be a 'work cleaner, sharper and more controlled' project.
