What Changed Since Yesterday

Military and Security Developments

IRGC Kinetic Attack on Commercial Shipping

Iranian Revolutionary Guard Corps conducted confirmed attack on Indian-flagged tanker Sanmar Herald on 18 April 2026. Vessel had reportedly received explicit Iranian transit clearance, yet was fired upon by IRGC fast attack craft, demonstrating critical command-and-control failure within Iranian forces.

Israeli Military Operations in Lebanon

Israeli airstrikes continued targeting suspected Hezbollah positions in southern Lebanon. Israel maintains public position that Lebanon is not covered by ceasefire arrangements, while Hezbollah has not acknowledged any ceasefire framework.

Houthi Threat Assessment

Houthi forces maintained elevated threat posture with statements indicating potential expansion of operations to target vessels in Bab el-Mandeb strait, creating credible threat of compound choke point disruption.

Political and Diplomatic Developments

Trump Administration Statements

President Trump cancelled planned envoy trip to Islamabad involving Jared Kushner and Steve Witkoff, citing inability to confirm Iranian attendance. Trump characterised Iran as being in "state of collapse" and demanded Hormuz opening linked to Iranian delivery of "unified proposal."

Iranian Diplomatic Position

Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi conducted intensive 72-hour diplomatic tour (Islamabad, Muscat, Moscow) producing proposal for Hormuz reopening in exchange for US blockade lifting with nuclear talks deferred. US Secretary Rubio explicitly rejected this framework.

Pakistan Mediation Status

Pakistan's mediation role formally intact but operationally stalled after cancellation of US envoy trip. No new talks scheduled as of 29 April, with Pakistani officials acknowledging "complexities" in bringing parties to table.

Shipping and Choke Point Status

Strait of Hormuz Traffic

Verified traffic data confirms severe sustained disruption: only 8 vessels transiting in 24 hours against normal flow of approximately 60. 45+ container vessels trapped within Persian Gulf with emergency surcharges of $1,500–4,000 per TEU.

Insurance Market Withdrawal

Major P&I clubs including Gard, Skuld, NorthStandard, London P&I Club, American Club, and Steamship Mutual have cancelled or severely restricted cover for Gulf operations. War-risk premiums elevated to 0.8–3.0% of hull value (6–20× pre-crisis).

LNG Carrier Rerouting

Minimal Qatari loadings at Ras Laffan with no confirmed laden carrier departures through Hormuz. Increased traffic to US Gulf Coast, Australian, and West African terminals as Asian buyers compete aggressively for non-Qatari supply.

Ceasefire Reality Check

Formal Ceasefire Terms

Start Date and Original Expiry

Ceasefire framework originated mid-April 2026 with explicit terms covering specific military fronts. Precise start date, original duration, and parties remain subjects of dispute between Israeli, Iranian, and US sources.

Parties to Agreement

No formal multilateral agreement with verification mechanisms established. Appears to reflect tacit understandings mediated through back channels rather than signed commitments, creating inherent fragility.

Verification Mechanisms

Verification relies primarily on satellite monitoring and intelligence reporting rather than on-site inspection or third-party observation, enabling both sides to claim compliance while continuing operations in excluded zones.

Scope Disputes and Exclusions

Lebanon Inclusion Status

Israel has explicitly and consistently stated that Lebanon is not covered by any ceasefire arrangement. This position is operationally validated by continued Israeli airstrikes in southern Lebanon targeting Hezbollah.

Hezbollah Non-Participation

Hezbollah has not agreed to and is not bound by any ceasefire. Organisation has maintained rocket fire into northern Israel throughout declared ceasefire period, though at reduced intensity compared to peak escalation.

Houthi Activity Outside Framework

Houthi forces in Yemen similarly outside any ceasefire framework, with Bab el-Mandeb threat representing independent escalation potential that could compound Hormuz disruption.
