Gas-intensive operations (food processing, chemicals, ceramics, glass). Energy as direct process input. Critical priority for fixing.

Data centers, cold storage, heavy industry. Proportional exposure to power price increases requires margin protection.
Priority Actions
Multi-Site Operators
Retail chains, hospitality groups. Portfolio approach recommended: fix some sites long, retain flexibility on others. Centralized procurement essential.
Hospitality & Care Sectors
24/7 operation requirements, regulatory temperature mandates. Service continuity protection critical. May qualify for essential service support.
SMEs & Charities
Limited procurement expertise, cash flow sensitivity. Use Switch 365 Ltd advisory services. Avoid default rollover to variable rates.
Critical Warning
Do not wait for supplier book updates. Supplier books update upward, not downward, in volatile markets. The "update" may be withdrawal from fixed pricing, not better prices. Each day of delay consumes the fixing window without benefit. Request quotes from multiple suppliers now and act on available information.

Critical Commercial Interpretation
The World Bank baseline transforms the delay calculation: even the "good outcome" (May easing) produces structurally higher prices (+24%). The "bad outcome" (prolongation) produces catastrophic prices. There is no scenario where waiting produces pre-crisis prices. The baseline makes delay more dangerous, not less.
