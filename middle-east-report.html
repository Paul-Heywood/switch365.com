Futures Market Explained

Why the Futures Market Matters

The futures market shows the price for energy delivered in future periods. A customer renewing in September 2027 is not only interested in today's price. They need to know what the market is currently offering for September 2027, 2028 and 2029 delivery.

If 2027, 2028 or 2029 delivery periods are cheaper than the current market, the customer may be able to use that future pricing now, depending on supplier availability and contract rules.

The risk with waiting is that those future periods move closer every day. When they become the near-term market, they can pick up the same risk premium that exists today.

Key Principle

Cheaper further out does not mean cheaper later.

The future market is only useful while the customer can still buy that future delivery period.

Waiting lets the market make the decision for the customer.

This is not automatic advice to fix. It is a reason to review live prices now.

Why NBP Matters to UK Businesses

NBP is the main UK wholesale gas benchmark. When NBP moves, supplier gas pricing moves. Because gas-fired generation still sets the marginal electricity price in many periods, NBP risk also flows through to electricity quotes.

Why UK Power Futures Matter

UK power futures show what the market offers for future delivery. A customer renewing in September 2027 should look at what the market is offering for September 2027, 2028 and 2029 — not just today's price.

Why Waiting Is Risky

Waiting does not guarantee access to today's cheaper future prices. As delivery periods move closer, they can absorb the same risk premium as the current market. Waiting allows the market to decide for the customer.

90-Day Out-of-Contract Risk

Flexible / pass-through contract holders are directly exposed to wholesale movement. All should review live pricing now.

Critical Distinction

Operational evidence matters.
