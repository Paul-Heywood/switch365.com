Executive Summary

World Bank Baseline Warning

The World Bank's April 2026 baseline projects energy prices 24% higher in 2026, with Brent crude averaging $86 per barrel, even under the explicit assumption that "the most acute disruptions end in May" and Hormuz shipping "gradually returns to pre-war levels by late 2026" [1014] [1045]. With May now underway and the assumed de-escalation endpoint showing no clear signs of arrival, this baseline is increasingly at risk of being undershot on price.

US Naval Enforcement

The US naval blockade of Iranian ports remains fully operational with at least 38 vessels directed to turn around since 13 April 2026 [738].

Political and Diplomatic Stalemate

The diplomatic track remains stalled on procedural preconditions. No Pakistan-mediated talks took place on 06–07 May 2026, with Iranian officials maintaining their position that they "have not decided to send negotiators to Pakistan while the blockade remained" [1014].

The ceasefire operates as an informal "rolling truce" without formal extension, verified mutual commitment, or progress toward comprehensive negotiation.

Shipping and Maritime Reality

Precise daily vessel traffic data for 06–07 May is not publicly available, but verified data shows just 191 vessels transited Hormuz in April 2026 compared to the normal monthly average of approximately 3,000 vessels—a reduction to roughly 6% of normal traffic [738].

War-risk insurance premiums remain at multi-year highs, with some shipowners continuing to route vessels around the Cape of Good Hope rather than through the Red Sea.

Repricing Window Risk

During extreme volatility, suppliers may suspend new fixed-price offers entirely rather than risk mispricing. This offer withdrawal risk is particularly acute for smaller customers.
