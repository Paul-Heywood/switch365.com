Key Price Drivers

🌍

LNG Supply

Strong LNG deliveries to Europe continue, with multiple cargoes arriving at UK and Continental terminals. Global LNG supply growth from the US and Qatar is easing competition for cargoes.

Bearish
💧

European Storage

EU gas storage is currently at 68% full, well ahead of the 5-year average. The strong injection season reduces fears of winter supply shortages and puts downward pressure on prices.

Bearish
⚡

Renewable Generation

Wind generation has been strong, averaging 35% of UK electricity supply. Higher renewable output reduces gas-for-power demand and eases pressure on prompt power prices.

Bearish
🛣

Norwegian Maintenance

Seasonal maintenance on Norwegian gas fields is ongoing but largely as planned. No major unplanned outages reported, keeping supply flows stable to the UK and Continental Europe.

Neutral
🌡

Temperature Outlook

Temperatures are forecast to be milder than seasonal normal for the coming 2 weeks, reducing heating demand. This should support continued strong storage injections.

Bearish
📈

Carbon Prices

EUA carbon prices have firmed slightly on market reforms speculation. Higher carbon prices support gas-fired generation economics relative to coal, indirectly supporting gas demand.

Slightly Bullish
📊

Market Further Out: Is the Market Cheaper Further Out?

The UK gas and power forward curves are currently in backwardation, meaning longer-dated contracts are trading at a discount to near-term prices. This structure presents potential hedging opportunities for flexible purchasers. The charts below show the full forward curves out to 36 months, clearly illustrating where the market sees value.
