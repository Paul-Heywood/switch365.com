Executive Summary

Overnight Market-Moving Developments

Diplomatic Breakdown

Trump's Conditional Extension: The 21 April Truth Social announcement extended the ceasefire indefinitely but explicitly maintained the naval blockade and military readiness, framing the extension as a response to Pakistan's request rather than US strategic choice.

Failed Islamabad Talks: Iranian negotiators refused to attend scheduled second-round talks, with Tasnim news agency citing US obstruction as the reason. Iranian parliamentary advisor Mohammad Baqer Qalibaf characterised the extension as "a ploy to buy time" for surprise attack.

Military Posturing

Continued US Blockade: Trump's extension announcement explicitly directed that "our Military to continue the Blockade and, in all other respects, remain ready and able," with 27 vessels turned back by 21 April.

Market Response

Limited Price Relief: The ceasefire extension produced muted market response, with oil prices stabilising rather than declining substantially, indicating traders view the extension as temporary pause rather than genuine de-escalation.

Supply Reality Unchanged: The Strait of Hormuz remained effectively closed with no significant change in vessel traffic patterns, maintaining the 95% reduction from normal commercial flow.
