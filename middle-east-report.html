Market Stabilisation vs Delayed Repricing Analysis

ANALYST INTERPRETATION: The ceasefire is not stabilising the market in any meaningful sense. It has achieved a partial, conditional pause in direct US-Iran hostilities that prevents immediate escalation to wider regional war, but it has not normalised shipping flows, resolved the nuclear issue, or stopped the Lebanon fighting.

The market is pricing the ceasefire as a delaying mechanism rather than a resolution pathway, with risk premium reflecting probability of eventual breakdown rather than confidence in diplomatic success. The World Bank's assessment that outcomes are "markedly tilted" toward higher prices reflects this interpretation.
