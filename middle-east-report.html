Executive Summary

Commercial Overview of Last 24 Hours

The 24-hour period from 06 May 2026 to 07 May 2026 delivered a deceptively quiet geopolitical backdrop that masked continued structural pressure on UK business energy pricing. No major new military incidents were independently verified across the Iran-Israel-US triangle, the Lebanon-Hezbollah front, or Houthi operations in Yemen. Yet energy markets exhibited significant intraday volatility and ultimately firming price action, with Brent crude recovering from a 06 May low near $101 to trade around $109 by the 07 May close, and UK NBP gas front-month futures climbing 3.5% to 111.35 pence per therm.

This pattern—quiet headlines, restless markets—reveals a critical commercial reality for Switch 365 Ltd's customer base: risk premiums are becoming structurally embedded in forward curves, not merely reacting to daily news flow.

World Bank Baseline in Customer Conversations

Critical Framing for Customer Conversations

"The World Bank tells us that even if everything goes right—the war eases in May, Hormuz reopens, shipping normalises by late 2026—energy prices still rise 24% this year. That's the good news scenario. The bad news scenario, which the World Bank says is more likely, is that the war drags on, disruption continues, and prices go higher still.

Fixing now doesn't mean you win if prices fall. It means you don't lose if they rise. In an environment where the most respected economic institution says the baseline is +24% with upside risk, not fixing is an active bet on prices falling—a bet that the World Bank says is against the odds."
