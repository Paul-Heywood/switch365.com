Executive Summary

The conditional US-Iran ceasefire announced April 8 collapsed within 24 hours, with the Strait of Hormuz confirmed fully closed again on April 9 after limited vessel passages. Israeli operations in Lebanon—explicitly excluded from ceasefire coverage—triggered Iranian reversal.

What Has Changed Since Yesterday

Military Developments

Israeli Operations in Lebanon

Israeli government confirmed Lebanon is excluded from ceasefire arrangements, with continued airstrikes on southern Lebanon and Bekaa Valley targeting Hezbollah infrastructure [3].

Iranian IRGC Operations

IRGC conducted missile and drone attacks against energy infrastructure in Kuwait, Bahrain, and UAE, with threats of "heavier and more massive" attacks [3].

Houthi Rebel Activity

Attacks on vessels in Red Sea and Gulf of Aden using anti-ship missiles, with expanded targeting criteria to include any Israeli, US, or UK commercial connections [3].

Strait of Hormuz Timeline

April 8: Conditional Opening

Trump announces two-week suspension of US attacks conditional on Hormuz reopening. Two oil tankers permitted transit under military supervision [4].

April 9: Full Closure Confirmed

Strait confirmed "fully closed" with tankers forced to turn back. Iranian Press TV reports oil tanker "AUROURA" executing 180-degree turn near Musandam coast [3].
