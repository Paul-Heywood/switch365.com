Critical Distinction: Hormuz vs. Iranian Ports

Access through the Strait of Hormuz to non-Iranian ports is different from access to Iranian ports. The US naval blockade (announced 13 April 2026) specifically targets Iranian coastline access, while Hormuz transit for neutral vessels continues under restrictions.

This distinction is commercially crucial: Hormuz disruption affects 20% of global oil supply, while Iranian port blockade primarily affects Iranian exports.

Impact on UK Business Energy Prices

Transmission Mechanisms

Gas Market Dynamics

UK gas prices follow European gas prices (TTF), which follow LNG availability. LNG disruption in the Gulf directly affects UK wholesale gas prices through global supply competition.

💡 Even though the UK buys from multiple sources, marginal LNG cargoes set the price.

Power Market Link

UK electricity prices are 85-90% correlated with gas prices due to gas-fired generation being the marginal price-setter in the UK power market.

💡 This explains why Middle East gas disruption affects UK electricity contracts.

Supplier Pricing Behavior

Price Book Lag

There is a lag between wholesale market repricing and supplier price-book updates. UK businesses face exposure during this window, especially those within 12 months of contract end.

Typical lag: 2-6 weeks
